Density of States (DOS) Visualisation

An intuitive and interactive exploration of how dimensionality affects the density of states of an ideal Fermi gas.

Python Statistical Mechanics

Overview

This project investigates how the density of states (DOS) depends on the dimensionality of an ideal Fermi gas. My goal here is to build intuition around phase space arguments rather than treating the DOS as a memorised formula. The description below is the way I tried explaining the concept of DOS to myself when revising for Solid State Physics.

We start with the "Free Electron Model" of a solid from high school chemistry, which introduces the idea that in a bulk material electrons are "free" to move around. Metallic materials are described as having a "sea of delocalized electrons", filled by electrons from the outermost shell, where electrons are free to move around within the bounds of the material. This surprisingly simple model is very appreciated in solid state physics and can be used to describe alkali metals, noble metals, and many more.

The question then becomes "what states do these electrons occupy?", and this is answered through solving the Schrödinger equation with periodic baundry conditions, which leads to plane wave solutions. The density of states function tells us how many distinct quantum states exist per energy slice. This video gives a very good and concise explanation of what and how the density of states is derived.
